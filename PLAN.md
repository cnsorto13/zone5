# NYC Marathon Training Plan
**Race:** NYC Marathon — November 2, 2026
**Goal:** Sub-4:00 (9:09/mi)
**Athlete:** Christian Sorto, 26, Alexandria VA

---

## The Real Goal

The marathon is the vehicle. The finish time is the standard. But the real goal is who you are on November 3rd.

Clear in your head. Patient. Proud. Fit and confident. Someone who went for something hard, didn't get talked out of it, and gave everything.

This plan is how you become that person.

---

## The Non-Negotiables

- **The plan does not get rebuilt every week.** Trust it. Deviations happen — that's normal. Rebuilding is the anxiety talking.
- **4 runs/week. Every week.** The schedule is the schedule.
- **Lifting scales with mileage** — not cut, just adjusted as the body needs more recovery.
- **Rest days are earned and guilt-free.** Do the work, take the rest. That's the contract.

---

## Weekly Structure

```
Mon  — Easy run + Legs
Tue  — REST (pure — golf, relax, nothing)
Wed  — Easy run + Push
Thu  — Tempo
Fri  — Pull only
Sat  — Long run
Sun  — REST (pure — nothing, blissful)
```

Two pure rest days. No optional shakeouts, no guilt. Tuesday and Sunday are yours.

---

## Training Phases

| Phase | Dates | Mileage | Focus |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 — Recovery + Reset | Apr 13 – Apr 27 | 15–18 mi/wk | Easy running only, let Cherry Blossom clear |
| 2 — Base Building | Apr 28 – Jun 8 | 18 → 28 mi/wk | Build the aerobic engine, nothing fancy |
| 3 — Marathon Specific | Jun 9 – Aug 2 | 28 → 38 mi/wk | Purposeful tempos, long runs 14–16 mi |
| 4 — Peak | Aug 3 – Sep 28 | 38 → 42 mi/wk | Long runs 17–20 mi, half marathon tune-up |
| 5 — Taper | Sep 29 – Nov 2 | 30 → 20 → race | Pull back, sharpen, arrive fresh |

**Mileage rules:**
- Never more than 10% increase week over week
- Every 3rd week is a cutback (~20% drop)
- Long run never more than 30% of weekly total

---

## Lifting by Phase

| Phase | Lifting |
|---|---|
| Phase 1–2 (Apr–Jun) | 3 days/week — full Push / Pull / Legs |
| Phase 3 (Jul–Aug) | 2 days/week — drop to Push + Pull, maintain Legs lightly |
| Phase 4 (Sep–Oct) | 1–2 days/week — maintenance only, protect the running |
| Taper | Optional — light or stop 10 days before race |

Do not sacrifice muscle. Scale back volume, not identity.

## Calorie Targets

*Starting point — track for 2 weeks, adjust based on energy and the scale.*

```
REST days (Tue, Sun)        → 2,200
Light lift day (Fri)        → 2,300
Training days (Mon, Wed)    → 2,600
Key days (Thu, Sat)         → 2,900–3,100
```

**The one rule:** eat light when you rest, eat big when it counts.

### Diet Phases

**Phase 1 — Strict Cut (Apr 20 – Jul 13, 12 weeks)**
- Hit the day targets above
- One drinking night per week max — 2–3 drinks, no binge eating after
- Never miss twice. One bad meal → next meal is normal. The spiral is the enemy, not the slip.
- Never arrive at a social event hungry. Pre-decide whether it's a drinking night.

**Phase 2 — Maintenance + Recomp (Jul 14 – Nov 2)**
- Eat at maintenance. Fuel the training. Let the miles do the work.
- 2–3 drink rule stays as the default but flexibility opens up.
- No aggressive cutting during taper or race week.

### Weight Projection
- Start: ~194 lbs
- After 12 weeks (Jul 13): ~182 lbs
- Race day estimate: 172–175 lbs

No goal weight. If 170–175 happens, it happens. The training drives it.

## The Social Rule

You can go out. You can enjoy the moment. But you decide in advance — not in the moment when the pressure is on.

The discipline isn't saying no to everything. It's deciding before you walk in the door.

---

## Rest Day Philosophy

If you hit your workouts, rest days are non-negotiable and guilt-free.

You don't earn rest by suffering through it. You earn it by doing the work. Recovery is where the adaptation happens — the run got better on the rest day, not during it.

A completed training week followed by two full rest days is perfect execution.

---

## What to Track (first 2 weeks)

- Daily calories vs. targets
- Energy levels on runs (especially tempo and long run)
- Weekly weigh-in (Monday morning, post-bathroom)
- How you feel on rest days

Adjust targets after week 2 based on real data.
